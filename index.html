What's the best way to avoid losing money?

Believe it or not, it is NOT putting your savings under the mattress.

Sophisticated investors and speculators use complicated strategies involving derivatives, options, and other exotic financial techniques. Those with enough money will invest it in a hedge fund that uses these and other techniques to avoid losing their money.

As recent history - 1987, 1998, 2000, 2008 - demonstrates, none of these strategies are entirely risk free.

So what if you don't have enough money to invest in a hedge fund? What if you do not want to use options, or forwards, or futures, or swaps in order to manage your risk? What if your largest nest egg is in an account that doesn't even offer these as a possibility?

This is the case for most people working towards retirement. They steadily save as much as they can over their working career, and invest it in tax deferred accounts such as Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs) or 401(k) company defined contribution retirement accounts. These kinds of accounts not only do not offer sophisticated security trading and hedging strategies, they are prevented from doing so by law.

Retirement investors that either do not want to or can not use sophisticated financial strategies still want to reduce the amount of their investments at risk. They just achieve this by a different means.

This is the type of investing that can benefit most from using the PennyKeeper service. You can track your investment performance and risk over time using the service. We can provide you the information to allocate your account to optimize the performance to risk ratio of the total portfolio. And you can do this by simply changing the amount you have in the different available investments.

FAQ

Does using this service mean I won't lose any money?
No! It means that you are less likely to lose money than if you don't.
Does using this service mean I will get a better return than anyone else?
No! It means that returns larger than yours are riskier (can lose more) than you.
What does Risk mean?
Here, it is the chance that you will lose money. It can be measured as a probability (percentage) or as an amount (value-at-risk).
What is Value-at-Risk (VaR)?
It is a measurement of risk that translates a percentage risk into a concrete dollar amount for your specific case.
